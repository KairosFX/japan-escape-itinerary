City Cards

One Card Per Stop

Each city card keeps the essentials in one place: top activity, must-try food, cash feel, and the reminder most likely to matter on the day.

Osaka

Neon Start

Top activity: Shinsekai and Tsutenkaku after dark. Must-try food: Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu, gyoza. Cash feel: 3,500 to 6,000 yen covers a casual food-heavy day well. Reminder: Keep Day 1 compact. Osaka works best when it feels easy.
Best first-night city
Kyoto

Cultural Contrast

Top activity: One focused temple and old-street cluster. Must-try food: Matcha sweets, soba, tofu, wagashi. Cash feel: 3,000 to 6,500 yen depending on how refined the meal gets. Reminder: One area beats a citywide chase. Wear walking shoes.
Long-walk day
Hakone

Scenic Reset

Top activity: Ryokan or onsen-region slow-down with one scenic stop. Must-try food: Ryokan dinner or early station-street snacks. Cash feel: 2,500 to 5,000 yen outside included lodging meals. Reminder: Bring one warm layer and buy snacks before things close.
Cooler than the cities
Mt. Fuji Area

Visibility Day

Top activity: Hit the clearest viewpoint first, then build the rest of Day 6 around it. Must-try food: Yoshida udon or a quick practical lunch near Kawaguchiko. Cash feel: 2,500 to 5,500 yen usually covers the day fine. Reminder: Weather still decides the order. Flexibility is part of the plan.
Coldest stop in the route
Tokyo

Clean Finish

Top activity: Shibuya shopping and a skyline close at Shibuya Sky. Must-try food: Sushi, tonkatsu, ramen, gyoza, dessert backup. Cash feel: 4,000 to 8,000 yen gives enough room for a nice final day. Reminder: Keep the day district-first so the ending stays sharp.
Easy last-day district
Transfer Days

Move Light

Top activity: Travel calmly, not ambitiously. Must-bring items: Charger, snacks, water, tickets, warm layer. Cash feel: Keep at least 10,000 yen accessible across the group for lockers, quick meals, and edge cases. Reminder: Agree on one station meet point before anyone splits off.
Day 4 matters most
Transit Cheats

Rushing-To-The-Station Version

This is the fast reference for the route's most useful movement notes.

Shin-Osaka -> Odawara Reserve the shinkansen ahead if you want a specific train. Budget about 2 hours on board plus station time.
Hakone Handoff Odawara to Hakone-Yumoto is the clean local switch. Sort bags and snacks early.
IC Card Rule Use one rechargeable IC card for local transit so Osaka, Kyoto, and Tokyo stay low-friction.
Quick Tips

Top 3 Etiquette And Safety Reminders

Quiet On Trains Keep voices low, let people off first, and avoid blocking doors or narrow aisles.
Read The Signs Temples, shrines, baths, and some food streets each have their own rules. Posted signs win.
Step Aside First Before checking maps, eating, or taking photos, move out of the main walking line.
Photo Prompts

One Shot To Keep Per City

  • Osaka: Upward angle in Shinsekai once the signs turn on.
  • Kyoto: Early or late on the Higashiyama slope before the crowd flattens the scene.
  • Hakone: Lakeside torii or shrine approach when the light softens.
  • Mt. Fuji Area: Get to the best clear viewpoint earlier than feels necessary.
  • Tokyo: Street energy first, skyline second at Shibuya after lights come on.
