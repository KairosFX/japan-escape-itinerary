Essentials

Essentials

Prepare before you fly

  • Check the entry rule for your passport on Japan’s MOFA visa page, and complete Visit Japan Web before departure so immigration/customs is smoother on arrival.
  • Arrange mobile data before landing: eSIM or pocket Wi‑Fi. JNTO says pocket Wi‑Fi is the safest option for guaranteed coverage, with airport pickup or hotel delivery available.
  • Bring a Type A plug adapter if needed; Japan uses 100V power.
  • Carry both a card and some cash. Cards are widely accepted in major cities, but JNTO notes Japan is still fairly cash-centric and smaller or more rural places may still prefer cash.

Book these in advance

  • Your Shin-Osaka → Odawara Shinkansen. SmartEX is the official online booking service for that route. If any suitcase is over 160 cm total dimensions, reserve a seat with oversized baggage space.
  • Your Hakone overnight stay, especially if it includes dinner or onsen access. If you have tattoos, check the bath policy first; JNTO notes many onsens still restrict tattoos, and standard etiquette is to wash before entering and not use swimwear in separate baths.
  • Shibuya Sky. The official site says tickets are sold ahead of time, popular slots can sell out, the rooftop may close in bad weather, and large bags are not allowed on the roof.
  • Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan. The official FAQ recommends e-tickets because timed entry can mean waits or sellouts on the day.

Transport prep for your exact route

  • Use one IC card for city travel in Osaka, Kyoto, and Tokyo. JNTO and JR West note major IC cards such as ICOCA/Suica/PASMO are widely interoperable on participating local networks and also work for small purchases, but your Shinkansen is a separate ticket/reservation.
  • For Hakone, the Odawara version of the Hakone Freepass fits your route better than the Shinjuku version, because you enter at Odawara and continue east afterward. Official Hakone guidance says the pass covers major Hakone transport and is designed for this kind of sightseeing flow.
  • Day 5 is the one to prep most carefully. Official Hakone/Fuji guidance shows a common Hakone → Kawaguchiko route is bus to Gotemba, then Fujikyu bus to Kawaguchiko, and that route is not covered by the JR Pass. Screenshot the timetable the night before.
  • Consider traveling light from Osaka through Hakone/Fuji. JNTO says luggage delivery/storage services are common, with size and weight limits, and they can save a lot of hassle on transfer-heavy days.

Pack for this itinerary

  • Comfortable walking shoes. Your biggest walking days are Kyoto and Fuji area.
  • Layers. Osaka/Tokyo are easy city days, but Hakone and Kawaguchiko mornings/evenings can feel cooler.
  • Compact umbrella or light rain shell.
  • Small daypack, power bank, water bottle, meds, and blister pads.
  • Easy-to-remove shoes and good socks. JNTO notes some attractions, restaurants, and traditional hotels will ask you to remove shoes.
  • A small overnight bag for Hakone is smarter than dragging a full suitcase everywhere.

Small itinerary-specific notes

  • For Chureito/Arakurayama, be ready for stairs: official Fujiyoshida tourism says the viewpoint involves 398 steps.
  • For Hakone, keep the pace relaxed rather than cramming in too many stops; the area is spread out.
  • For your Tokyo night, do not bring a bulky bag to Shibuya Sky.

Notes メモ

Trip Notes 旅のメモ

Short notes explaining each stop on the trip. 旅の各立ち寄り先を短くまとめたメモです。

Osaka 大阪

Starting base of the trip and the introduction to Japan’s food and nightlife culture. The focus here is on atmosphere, street food, and exploring the city rather than packing in too many attractions. Dotonbori is the main highlight and can be enjoyed both during the day and at night. During the day it is good for exploring street food stalls, walking along the canal, and visiting nearby Shinsaibashi shopping street, while nighttime is when the area becomes especially lively with neon lights, crowds, and nightlife. Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan, scheduled for Day 3, is one of the largest aquariums in the world and offers a slower sightseeing experience before leaving Osaka. The remaining time in the city can be kept flexible for additional food exploration, shopping, or revisiting Dotonbori. 旅の出発地点で、日本の食文化とナイトライフに触れる最初の街です。ここでは観光地を詰め込みすぎず、街の雰囲気、食べ歩き、街歩きを楽しむことを重視します。主な見どころは道頓堀で、昼も夜も楽しめます。昼は食べ歩きや川沿いの散策、近くの心斎橋商店街を回るのに向いており、夜になるとネオンや人の賑わいで特に活気が増します。3日目に予定している海遊館は世界最大級の水族館の一つで、大阪を離れる前にゆったり観光できるスポットです。残りの時間は、さらに食べ歩きをしたり、買い物をしたり、もう一度道頓堀を訪れたりと柔軟に使えます。

Kyoto 京都

Planned as a full-day trip from Osaka. Kyoto represents the cultural and historic portion of the trip and provides a strong contrast to Osaka’s modern nightlife atmosphere. The main areas planned are Arashiyama, known for its bamboo grove, scenic river, and mountain views, and Nanzen-ji Temple, a large Zen temple complex with quiet gardens and historic architecture. Kyoto is dense with temples and shrines, so the focus here is on a few major areas rather than trying to see everything in one day. 大阪からの日帰り終日観光として計画しています。京都は旅の中で文化と歴史を感じるパートで、大阪の現代的でナイトライフ中心の雰囲気とは対照的です。主な予定地は、竹林、川辺の景色、山の眺めで知られる嵐山と、静かな庭園や歴史ある建築を持つ大きな禅寺の南禅寺です。京都は寺社が非常に多いため、1日で全てを見ようとせず、主要なエリアをいくつかに絞って回る想定です。

Hakone 箱根

First major stop when heading east. Travel from Shin-Osaka to Odawara by bullet train, then continue into Hakone for sightseeing and an overnight stay. Hakone is known for its mountain scenery, hot springs, and potential views of Mt. Fuji on clear days, making it a good transition from the city portion of the trip into a slower, more scenic part of the itinerary. This stop is best approached at a relaxed pace, with time for sightseeing in the afternoon, an overnight stay, and a quieter morning before continuing on. The area is spread out, so travel between sights can take time, but that is part of the experience. A stay here is less about fitting in many attractions and more about enjoying the landscape, the atmosphere, and the chance to unwind before transferring toward the Mt. Fuji area on Day 5. 東へ向かう最初の大きな立ち寄り先です。新大阪から小田原まで新幹線で移動し、その後は箱根へ入って観光し、一泊します。箱根は山の景色、温泉、晴れた日の富士山の眺めで知られており、都市中心の旅程から、よりゆったりした景色重視の流れへ移るのにちょうどよい場所です。この滞在は、午後の観光と一泊、そして翌朝の静かな時間を含めて、落ち着いたペースで楽しむのが向いています。エリアは広く、各スポット間の移動に時間がかかることもありますが、それも体験の一部です。ここでの滞在は、多くの観光地を詰め込むよりも、風景や空気感を味わい、5日目に富士山エリアへ向かう前にひと息つくことに意味があります。

Mt. Fuji Area 富士山エリア

This portion of the trip focuses on scenery and classic views of Mount Fuji. The main base is around Lake Kawaguchiko, one of the best viewing locations for the mountain. Planned stops include Oshino Hakkai, a small village known for its clear spring-fed ponds and traditional atmosphere, as well as Arakurayama Sengen Park, home to the famous Chureito Pagoda viewpoint that overlooks Mt. Fuji and the surrounding town. Because Fuji visibility depends heavily on weather conditions, this part of the itinerary should remain flexible so the best viewpoints can be prioritized if the mountain is visible. この区間は、富士山の景色と定番の眺望を楽しむためのパートです。主な拠点は河口湖周辺で、富士山を見るのに特に人気の高い場所のひとつです。予定している立ち寄り先は、湧水池と昔ながらの雰囲気で知られる忍野八海と、富士山と町並みを見渡せる有名な忠霊塔の展望で知られる新倉山浅間公園です。富士山の見え方は天候に大きく左右されるため、この部分は柔軟にして、見えているときに最も良い展望地を優先できるようにするのがおすすめです。

Tokyo 東京

Final city stop of the trip and the most urban portion of the itinerary. The focus is kept simple to avoid overloading the schedule. The main area explored is Shibuya, which includes the famous Shibuya Crossing, surrounding shopping and entertainment streets, and Shibuya Sky, a rooftop observation deck that provides wide views of Tokyo and is especially impressive at night. This stop serves as a lively conclusion to the trip after the scenic Fuji portion. 旅の最後の都市で、最も都会的なパートです。予定を詰め込みすぎないよう、内容はシンプルにしています。主に回るのは渋谷で、有名な渋谷スクランブル交差点、その周辺の買い物・娯楽エリア、そして東京を広く見渡せる屋上展望台の渋谷スカイが中心です。特に夜の景色が印象的で、景色重視だった富士山パートのあとを締めくくるにぎやかな終着点になります。

Extra Tokyo Days 東京の追加日程

If additional days are added, the itinerary expands to include other major Tokyo districts. Tokyo Skytree and the nearby Tokyo Solamachi complex provide another observation point and a large shopping and dining area. Akihabara offers a completely different atmosphere focused on anime, gaming, electronics, and pop culture. Shinjuku provides dense city views, nightlife, and major transportation hubs, while the Tokyo Imperial Palace area offers a quieter historic landmark surrounded by large gardens in the center of the city. 日数を追加できる場合は、東京の他の主要エリアも回る想定です。東京スカイツリーと隣接する東京ソラマチは、別の展望スポットと大きな商業・飲食エリアとして楽しめます。秋葉原はアニメ、ゲーム、家電、ポップカルチャーを中心にしたまったく違う雰囲気があり、新宿は高密度な都市景観、ナイトライフ、大きな交通拠点が魅力です。皇居周辺は、都心にありながら広い庭園に囲まれた落ち着いた歴史的ランドマークとして加えられます。

Future Trip 次回の旅

Okinawa is kept separate for a future visit. Its tropical island environment, beaches, and relaxed pace make it very different from the mainland cities and mountain regions included in this itinerary. 沖縄は今後の別旅行として分けて考えます。亜熱帯の島の雰囲気、ビーチ、ゆったりした空気感は、本行程に含まれる本州の都市や山岳エリアとは大きく異なるためです。
