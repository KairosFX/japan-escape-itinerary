7-Day Japan Route

City Nights, Fuji Views, Tokyo Finish

This plan starts in Osaka for atmosphere, food, and nightlife, folds in a full Kyoto cultural day, moves east through Hakone, keeps the Mt. Fuji section flexible for weather, and finishes with a simple Tokyo landing focused on Shibuya.

Route Osaka -> Kyoto -> Osaka -> Shin-Osaka -> Odawara -> Hakone -> Mt. Fuji Area -> Tokyo
Trip Style

Keep Osaka relaxed, use Kyoto as a full-day contrast, and avoid overpacking the schedule early.

Transit Anchor

Day 4 is the main transfer day: bullet train from Shin-Osaka to Odawara, then into Hakone for the overnight.

Flex Priority

The Mt. Fuji area works best when the weather stays clear, so leave Day 6 loose enough to adapt.

Core Stops

What Each Stop Is For

The itinerary stays compact on purpose: fewer cities, stronger contrast, and enough breathing room for food, atmosphere, and transit shifts.

Osaka

Use Osaka as the starting base. Prioritize city atmosphere, late evenings, street food, Tsutenkaku, Shinsekai, and Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan.

Nightlife Food-first pacing Kaiyukan

Kyoto

Best handled as a full-day trip from Osaka. This is the cultural and historic counterweight to the looser city energy of Osaka.

Day trip Historic feel Different pace

Hakone + Fuji

Hakone is the first eastern stop after the bullet train to Odawara. From there, shift toward the Mt. Fuji area for the most scenic stretch of the trip.

Onsen-region feel Transit pivot Weather-dependent views

Tokyo

Keep Tokyo simple on the main 7-day version: enter through Shibuya, browse the shopping areas, and end with Shibuya Sky at night.

Shibuya Crossing Shopping Night skyline
