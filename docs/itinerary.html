Arrival base

Osaka sets the opening mood

The trip starts in Osaka because it is the easiest place to land, eat, and get into Japan's food-and-nightlife rhythm without adding immediate logistics friction.

Nightly Stays

Where You Sleep

This keeps the route shape visible without opening every daily card: Osaka x3, Hakone x1, Fuji area x1, then Tokyo as the final city anchor.

Nights 1 to 3 Osaka base for the arrival night, the full Kyoto day trip, and the Kaiyukan plus Osaka Castle reset day.
Night 4 Hakone overnight with Ropeway or Owakudani, onsen or ryokan time, and a slower pace before Fuji.
Night 5 Fuji area, ideally near Kawaguchiko, so Day 6 can react to actual visibility instead of wishful timing.
Final Base Tokyo. Keep the last leg centralized in Shibuya or another easy district so the finish stays simple.

Move Days

Fast Transit Reference

This is the part to open when someone only wants the route handoff and not the whole planning story.

Osaka -> Kyoto Simple day-trip logic. Go early enough for Arashiyama and the Higashiyama side, then return to Osaka instead of moving hotels.
Shin-Osaka -> Odawara Roughly 2 hours on board, plus calm station time. This is the route's main booking day.
Odawara -> Hakone-Yumoto Short local handoff once the shinkansen work is done. Transfer first, then decide between Ropeway, Owakudani, or ryokan time.
Hakone -> Fuji Area Use the morning for Lake Ashi and Hakone Shrine, then continue toward Kawaguchiko with enough energy left for Day 6.
Fuji Area -> Tokyo About the 2-hour zone on direct options or bus-style returns. That is why the final Tokyo day stays concentrated in Shibuya.
Main Guardrail Fuji visibility matters more than forcing a rigid scenic order, and transit days win by staying clean.
