Nightly Stays

Where You Sleep

This keeps the route shape visible without opening every daily card: Osaka x3, Hakone x1, Fuji x1, then Tokyo as the final city anchor.

Nights 1 to 3 Osaka base. Use it for the arrival, Kyoto day trip, and the easy reset day before moving east.
Night 4 Hakone. This is the scenic hinge that breaks up the longest travel shift.
Night 5 Fuji area. Stay close enough that Day 6 can react to actual visibility instead of wishful timing.
Final Base Tokyo. Keep the last leg centralized in Shibuya or another easy district so the finish stays clean.
Day Flow

Seven Days, Cleanly Ordered

Fast-skim version first, then move-day details below.

Day 1 / Osaka Land, settle in, then do one strong neon-and-food walk. Keep it compact and forgiving. Arrival mood
Day 2 / Kyoto One focused cultural day. Pick a lane and let the city change the mood without becoming a scattershot. One full contrast day
Day 3 / Osaka Kaiyukan or another easy anchor, then food, light wandering, and packing reset before transit day. Buffer day
Day 4 / Osaka To Hakone Main shinkansen day: Shin-Osaka to Odawara, then the Hakone handoff. Protect the transfer first. Big move day
Day 5 / Hakone To Fuji Slow morning, one more scenic moment if you want it, then move toward the Fuji area with energy left. Do not overforce it
Day 6 / Fuji Scenic Day Check the sky, cash in the clearest viewpoint first, then layer Kawaguchiko or Oshino Hakkai around it. Visibility-led
Day 7 / Tokyo Keep the day district-first in Shibuya: shopping, dinner, and skyline finish instead of cross-city overload. Clean ending

Move Days

Fast Transit Reference

This is the part to open when someone just wants the route handoff and not the whole planning story.

Osaka -> Kyoto Simple day-trip logic. Go early enough for a full day, come back to Osaka instead of moving hotels.
Shin-Osaka -> Odawara Roughly 2 hours on board, plus calm station time. This is the route's main booking day.
Odawara -> Hakone-Yumoto Short local handoff once the shinkansen work is done. Sort bags first.
Hakone -> Fuji Area Do not turn this into a racing day. Keep enough energy for actual Day 6 visibility use.
Fuji Area -> Tokyo About the 2-hour zone on direct options or bus-style returns. That is why Tokyo stays district-first in the base plan.
Main Guardrail Transit days win by being clean, not by squeezing in maximum extra sights around them.
